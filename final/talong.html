Tortang Talong

Tortang talong is a Filipino eggplant omelette that transforms a simple vegetable into a culinary masterpiece. This beloved dish begins with a whole eggplant charred over an open flame until smoky and tender, then delicately dipped in beaten eggs and pan-fried to golden perfection1. Originating from the rich culinary traditions of the Philippines, tortang talong reflects the country's history of Chinese influence and local innovation, with roots tracing back to the 16th century when Chinese traders first introduced cooking techniques to the archipelago. The result is a humble yet extraordinary dish - crispy on the outside, soft and creamy on the inside - typically served with steaming white rice and a side of tangy banana ketchup. So irresistible that it was once crowned the world's best egg dish, tortang talong is a testament to the magic that happens when simple ingredients are prepared with love and cultural pride.

Recipe at a glance:
Ingredients Quantity Unit of Measurement
Chinese Eggplant 4 pieces
Eggs 2 pieces
Salt 1 teaspoon
Oil 6 tablespoons
